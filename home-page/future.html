1400s - 1800s: Trans-Atlantic Slave Trade

Portugese, Spanish, Dutch, English, and French ships began to transport enslaved Africans for labor along the west coast of Africa to the Carribean, North, and South America. The Middle Passage was the route in which ships travelled as many as 300 miles for 2 months. Over 15 million Africans were enslaved and displaced.

1863: Emancipation Proclamation

U.S. President Abraham Lincoln issued freedom of enslaved people in the Confederate States during the Civil War. Two years later, the news reached enslaved communities in Texas, inspiring the holiday Juneteenth. However, many African Diasporic people were share-croppers and still performed the same work for cheap labor.

1916 - 1970: Great Migration

Millions of African Diasporic people moved from the Southern states to the North and Western states to pursue economic oppurtunities and escape from racial violence such as lynchings and church attacks.
