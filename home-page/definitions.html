Definitions, references, and other common terms used in this website.

African Diasporic: a term to refer to the African population of displaced or scattered people with a common hertiage or homeland, residing in various geographic locations.

Black: a racialized term used to divide African Diasporic people into catergoies in America and abroad; based on skin color and appearance, but also used to justify discrimination based on race.

Chattel Slavery: enslaved people were the personal property of their owners for life, a source of labor or a commodity that could be willed, traded or sold like livestock or furniture.

Colonialism: the project of European political domination that began in the early sixteenth century.

Glitch: a sudden, usually temporary malfunction or irregularity of equipment; unexpcted snag in the system, machine, or plan; African artists and developers in a colonized white hegemonic field.

Race: a construct based on physical appearance rather than culture, heritage, or ethnicity; created by colonizers (i.e. white people vs black people).

West Africa: region in Africa where the Trans-Atlantic slave trade took place. Modern day countries include Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, and Côte d'Ivoire.
